About BearAware Romania

Building bridges between technology, wildlife, and communities for a sustainable future

Our Vision

A future where humans and wildlife coexist harmoniously, supported by intelligent technology that protects both communities and ecosystems.

BearAware Romania was founded in 2023 in response to increasing human-wildlife conflicts in the Carpathian Mountains. What started as a research project has evolved into a comprehensive conservation technology platform that combines artificial intelligence, real-time monitoring, and community engagement to create safer outcomes for both people and bears.

Our work addresses a critical challenge: Romania is home to Europe's largest brown bear population, with over 6,000 individuals. While these magnificent creatures are vital to the ecosystem, expanding human settlements and recreational activities have led to more frequent encounters, sometimes with tragic consequences.

The Challenge We Address

Human-Wildlife Conflict in the Carpathians

The Carpathian Mountains, stretching across Romania, are one of Europe's last great wilderness areas. This rugged landscape supports not only brown bears, but also wolves, lynx, and countless other species. However, as human activities expand into these areas, conflicts have become increasingly common.

  • Increasing Encounters: Tourism and hiking in the Carpathians have grown dramatically, bringing more people into bear territory.
  • Property Damage: Bears seeking food sometimes damage property, creating economic hardship for rural communities.
  • Safety Concerns: While attacks are rare, the fear of encounters can limit recreational access to these beautiful mountains.
  • Bear Mortality: Approximately 150 bears are killed annually due to conflicts, threatening the long-term sustainability of the population.

Our Solution

Technology meets conservation

1

AI-Powered Detection

Using YOLOv8, one of the world's most advanced computer vision models, our system can identify bears in video streams with 98% accuracy. The model runs on edge devices deployed throughout the Carpathians, processing frames in real-time without requiring constant internet connectivity.

2

Instant Alert System

When a bear is detected, our Firebase-based alert system immediately notifies users in the affected area. Threat levels are automatically classified based on confidence scores, proximity, and bear behavior, ensuring appropriate responses.

3

Mobile Accessibility

Our mobile application provides interactive maps showing real-time bear locations, danger zones, and safe routes. Users can report sightings, access educational resources, and receive AI-powered patrol suggestions for rangers.

4

Data-Driven Insights

Every detection contributes to a growing database of bear behavior and movement patterns. This data helps researchers understand habitat use, seasonal patterns, and the effectiveness of conservation interventions.

Our Impact

Measurable results in conservation and safety

Zero Serious Incidents

Since launching our mobile app, we've had zero serious bear-related incidents among users who followed our safety guidelines and alert notifications.

Improved Coexistence

Local communities report increased confidence when hiking and reduced fear, leading to better quality of life and continued support for bear conservation.

Protected Wildlife

By preventing conflicts, we've contributed to the protection of Romania's brown bear population and their critical mountain habitat.

Extend our detection network to cover additional areas of the Carpathian Mountains, including cross-border regions in Ukraine and Slovakia.

Adapt our AI models to detect other large carnivores like wolves and lynx, creating a comprehensive wildlife monitoring system.

Open-source our technology and share our learnings with conservation organizations worldwide facing similar human-wildlife conflict challenges.
