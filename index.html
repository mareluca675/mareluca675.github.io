Protecting Romania's Wildlife Heritage

Romania is home to over 6,000 brown bears—Europe's largest population. The Carpathian Mountains provide a critical habitat for these magnificent creatures, but increasing human-wildlife interactions pose risks to both communities and bears.

BearAware combines artificial intelligence, real-time monitoring, and community engagement to create a safer coexistence. Our YOLOv8-powered detection system identifies bears with 98% accuracy, providing instant alerts to protect hikers, residents, and wildlife alike.

Conservation Education

Understanding bears is key to coexistence

About Romanian Brown Bears

Romania hosts Europe's largest brown bear population—over 6,000 individuals in the Carpathian Mountains. These keystone species are vital to ecosystem health.

  • Weight: Males up to 350 kg, females up to 200 kg
  • Lifespan: 20-25 years in the wild
  • Habitat: Dense Carpathian forests

Safety & Prevention

Most bear encounters can be avoided by following simple guidelines and staying aware of your surroundings.

  • Make Noise: Talk, sing, or clap regularly on trails
  • Travel in Groups: More people = safer hiking
  • Be Aware: Watch for signs, stay alert at dawn/dusk
  • Store Food Properly: Use bear-resistant containers

Identifying Bear Signs

Recognizing signs of bear activity helps you stay aware and avoid surprise encounters.

  • Tracks: Five toes with visible claw marks
  • Scat: Large droppings with berries or hair
  • Claw Marks: Deep parallel gouges on trees
  • Feeding Signs: Torn logs, dug-up ground
